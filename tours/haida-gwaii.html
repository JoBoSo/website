Trip Report

      The ferry from Prince Rupert to Skidgate Landing on Haida Gwaii took eight hours. When I got off the boat, I biked north on the east coast of the island. Just about everyone I passed along the road was picking blackberries and thimbleberries. The ocean breeze was hitting hard. I camped on the beach and had a fire. There was a long, soft pink sunset. What more does a bicycle tourist need?

      The next morning, I continued north toward Port Clements. There was one little shop in town and it didn't have much, but everything that it did have was good. I ate some fresh cherry tomatoes and candy outside of the store and everyone who passed me on their way in started a friendly conversation. The Masset Inlet is quite a feature. It's equal parts ocean and land. It changes with the tide.

      After enjoying Port Clements, I rode south on the main forest service roads that connects Port Clements to Queen Charlotte. The roads were dark and cut through a tall, wet forest. The lakes along the roads were not very accessible and were quite swampy. The Golden Spruce Trail was along one of these roads and it had some of the biggest trees I've seen.

      I arrived in Queen Charlotte late in the evening, but the sun doesn't set until about 10pm in the summer. I camped on the edge of town by a cove. It wasn't a great night because it was so sticky and swampy. I should have returned to the beach north of Skidgate. When I woke up in the morning, I hung out in town for the day before returning to Skidgate to get on the return ferry.
