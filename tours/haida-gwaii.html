Trip Report

      When I was living in and around Smithers, BC, I drove toward Prince Rupert, camped at Prudhomme Provincial Park for a night, and woke up early the next morning to take the ferry to Skidgate Landing on Haida Gwaii with my Giant Trance X 3. The ferry ride was about eight hours. When I arrived, I biked north on the east coast. Just about everyone I passed was picking blackberries and thimbleberries along the road. The ocean breeze was blowing and everything was beautiful and clean. It felt great! I camped on the beach and had a fire. There was a long soft-pink sunset. It was everything I hoped to achieve.

      The next morning, I continued north toward Port Clements. There was one little shop in town and it didn't have much, but everything that it did have was really great. There were local vegetables, like cherry tomatoes, and sandwiches, desserts, and candy. I was sitting out front of the shop eating and everyone who passed me on their way in started a friendly conversation. The Masset Inlet is quite a feature. It's equal parts ocean and land.

      After enjoying Port Clements, I rode south on the main forest service roads that connect Port Clements to Queen Charlotte. The roads were dark and surrounded by tall, wet forest. The lakes along the roads were not very accessible and were quite swampy. The Golden Spruce Trail was along one of these roads and it had some of the biggest trees I've seen.

      I arrived in Queen Charlotte late in the evening, but the sun doesn't set until like 10pm in the summer. I camped on the edge of town by a cove. It wasn't a great night because it was so sticky and swampy. I should have returned to the beach north of Skidgate. When I woke up in the morning, I hung out in town before returning to Skidgate to get on the return ferry.
