Trip Report

      The P'tit Train du Nord is a 200 km paved and gravel rail trail that starts in Saint Jerome, just north of Montreal, and ends in Mont Laurier. The trail is mostly flat, but it runs through the Laurentian mountains, which tend to be in the range of 400-800 meters tall. My favorite section is between Saint Adele and Mont Tremblant where there are a few large lakes and granite cliffs in sight. Val David is a beautiful town along this section of the trail with some of the tallest trees I have seen in this part of Quebec.

      To north of Mont Tremblant, the trail and towns becomes quieter. The old train stations along the trail have water, washrooms, information, and often food. You could get by without packing food since there are so many grocery stores and restaurants along the way. There are also lots of potential spots along the trail to camp, so this tour can be done without much planning.

      I started my tour of the P'tit Train du Nord at a cottage I was living in between St Agathe and Mont Tremblant. At the end of my first day, I camped about 20kms south of Mont Tremblant next to a shelter along the trail. The next day, I biked to Mont Laurier and then back to the cottage. I took the third day off before riding out to Saint Jerome and back on the fourth day. It was an easy ride and I saw a lot of deer and fox.
