Trip Report

      The P'tit Train du Nord is a 200 km paved and gravel rail trail to the north of Montreal that starts in Saint Jerome and ends in Mont Laurier. The trail is mostly flat, but it runs through the Laurentian mountains, which tend to be in the range of 400-800 meters tall. My favorite section is between Saint Adele and Mont Tremblant since this is the busiest and most scenic stretch, with a few large lakes and granite cliffs in sight. Val David is a beautiful town with some of the tallest trees I have seen in this part of Quebec. To the north of Mont Tremblant, the trail becomes quiet and the towns less touristy and more rural. The old train stops along the trail provide water, washrooms, information, and often food. You could get by without packing food since there are so many grocery stores and restaurants along the way. There are also lots of private spots to pitch a tent and camp, so this tour can be done without much planning.

      I started my tour of the P'tit Train du Nord on my Kona Rove at a cottage I was renting between St Agathe and Mont Tremblant. At the end of my first day, I camped about 20 km south of Mont Tremblant next to a shelter along the trail. The next day, I biked to Mont Laurier and then back to the cottage. I took the third day off before riding out to Saint Jerome and back on the fourth day. It was easy riding and I saw plenty of deer and fox.
