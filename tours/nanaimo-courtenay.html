Trip Report

      When I drove to Courtenay from Ontario, I rented a car. I had to drop it off in Nanaimo, however. So, I biked to Courtenay from Nanaimo by cutting toward Port Alberni in the center of The Island then taking the logging roads to Courtenay. The first half of my ride was on paved highway. I camped along the river just outside of Port Alberni, but only for about two hours. I woke up when I got a weird feeling from my air mattress. I was floating on a shallow bed of water—the river was tidal. I leaned a little to far to one side and flooded my sleeping bag.

      At midnight, I packed up and put on my headlight. I continued on to the logging roads that I saw on Google Maps. I had no idea what a logging road was and figured biking on them would be just like riding dirt roads in Southern Ontario. Of course, it was more like riding on loose, fist-sized rocks that led up and down the sides of mountains. I was kind of scared. It was cold, dark, and the terrain was rough and foreign to me. However, I learned a lot about bike touring in the mountains.
