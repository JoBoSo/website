Trip Report

      My trip to Texada Island began by taking the ferry from Comox to Powell River. From Powell River, I biked north to Dinner Rock Rec Site where I saw a bear and her two cubs run through the forest on my way in. Dinner Rock is one of my favorite campsites. It’s on the ocean and you can watch the sun set slowly behind The Island. The next morning, I biked back to Powell River and took the ferry to Texada. I spent two nights on the island. I camped at Shingle Beach the first night and Bob’s Lake the second night. Bob’s Lake was a magical spot. The trail around the lake was surrounded by dry, spongy moss and there were lots of different types of mushrooms. There was an old stable falling apart in the forest. Gillies Bay is a beautiful town with colorful cottages along the ocean and big gardens. I sat on some bluffs that overlooked the ocean for lunch one day and an eagle flew above me. It was so quite that I could hear it’s wings cutting through the air. I biked as far south on the island as the road would allow me. Before I left, I ate at Mary Mary Café in Van Anda.
