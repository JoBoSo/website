Trip Report

      Cortes Island is my favorite island. Everything about it can be described as psychedelic, especially Mansons Landing. When I arrived at the beach in Mansons Landing, I saw a bunch of pontoon boats sitting on the rocky floor of an empty tidal lake to one side of the beach with cottages sitting on cliffs above them. I was so confused until I saw the ocean fill it back up with water and bring the pontoons with it later that day. In the ocean-bay to the other side of the beach were a bunch of anchored sailboats and people were having great times. It was hanging around docks and sailors on Cortes Island where I learned why sailors bother to come to shore at all. Eventually they run out of liquor and bags to fill with empty bottles. A typical dock-side scene involves a grey-haired man in a dirty white tee-shirt walking off his boat and across the dock with a couple boxes full of empty bottles while his wife trails behind him with a bag full of garbage. A few minutes later, the scene repeats in reverse, except the bottles are full and the wife is carrying groceries. I hope to have a sailboat one day.

      The village of Mansons Landing is also amazing. There was a café with a big porch across the front where three men with dreadlocks were smoking a bong like it was everybody’s business. Across the street, there was an organic farm with a beautiful geometric garden with lots of purple flowers. At the tables beside that garden were some middle-aged women dressed in black clothes, looking kind of like witches, talking to each other about crystals or something like that. It was quite a bustling and atypical village. There’s so much more I could say about Cortes, but the last I’ll say of it is that the ferry to Cortes Island is the best ferry in BC. The man who sold me my ticket wore a classic navy-blue and white sailor's cap and suit and the sailor manning the deck wore an orange jump suit with the top down and a white beater, showing his tattoos all up his arms. They were a couple of proper sailors.

      Quadra was nice as well, but I didn’t get much of a sense of what the community was like. It’s more developed than Cortes. The highlight of Quadra was seeing all the sailboats floating in the bay by Rebecca Spit because they were little white specks surrounded by dark blue ocean with the mountains on the sunshine coast in the background. On my way back to Campbell River from Quadra, I got the two-piece ling-cod dinner from the fish and chips restaurant by the dock and ate it on the ferry in the presence of Her Majesty, The Queen of England’s picture, which hangs on every ferry I’ve been on in BC to protect travellers from the whims of the Pacific. God bless her.
