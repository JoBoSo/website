Trip Report

      Cortes Island is my favorite island. It is magical. When I arrived at the beach in Mansons Landing, I saw a bunch of pontoon boats sitting on the rocky floor of an empty tide pool to one side of the beach with cottages sitting on cliffs above them. I was so confused until I saw the ocean fill it back up with water and bring the pontoons with it later that day. In the bay to the other side of the beach were a bunch of anchored sailboats and people lounging. In the main section of the village, there was a café with a big porch across the front where three guys with dreadlocks were smoking a bong like it was everybody’s business. Across the street, there was an organic farm with a beautiful symmetrical garden with lots of purple flowers. At the tables beside that garden were some middle-aged women dressed in black clothes, looking kind of like witches. It was quite bustling.

      Quadra was nice as well. It’s more developed than Cortes. The highlight of Quadra was seeing the sailboats floating in the bay by Rebecca Spit because they were little white specks surrounded by dark blue ocean with the mountains on the sunshine coast in the background. On my way back to Campbell River from Quadra, I got the two-piece ling-cod dinner from the fish and chips restaurant by the dock and ate it on the ferry in the presence of Her Majesty, The Queen of England’s picture, which hangs on every ferry I’ve been on in BC to protect travellers from the whims of the Pacific. God bless her.
