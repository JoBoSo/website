Trip Report

      A few days ago, I packed up and biked from my home in Montreal to Sherbrooke and back. The ride spanned three days and 399 kms of the Route Verte and other connecting paths. Quebec has the highest quality and quantity of bike routes I’ve seen. I was riding along smooth pavement and gravel paths that run through beautiful towns, forests, and farms. The trail through Yamaska National Park was lined with potential campsites along the edge of the lake and forest. I found the landscapes I was pursing once I reached Waterloo. It was at this point in the ride that the rolling hills became a constant. The balance between forest and farm was perfect. At the end of my first day, I camped on some mowed grass next to a power grid in the country.

      On my second day, I went through Mont Orford National Park. It was fun riding through the forest and there is a dedicated cyclist campground right off the trail, away from the car campgrounds. I got to Sherbrooke around 2pm where I ate shawarma, drank bubble tea, and toured the city. Then, I turned back around. I ate more shawarma for dinner in Magog and rode by the lake at sunset. Magog was my favorite town on the tour—the trail runs along the waterfront and lots of thought has been put toward making it a beautiful place. I pushed on in the dark to the cyclist campground in Mont Orford National Park for the night.

      My final day was a grind through the cold rain to get back home. Had the weather been good, my intention was to take another two days to go south from Sherbrooke to Stanstead on the Vermont border.
