Schomberg to Montreal

Distance: 615 km | Days: 6 | Terrain: Paved bike paths | Dates: Aug 16-21, 2019



Trip Report

      My first bike tour. I started in Schomberg, Ontario and Biked to Montreal via the Waterfront Trail. I had to ride through suburban Toronto before I reached the Waterfront Trail. This is the worst place to ride a bike, camp, and be outside. Roads are busy, it’s flat, and buildings look ugly. When I reached Darlington Provincial Park on Lake Ontario, the ride became nice, but my campsite cost $50. On the second day, I rode through towns along Lake Ontario. The road through Whitby took me by beautiful old houses and the marshy landscape around Presqu’ile Provincial Park was nice. In Prince Edward County, I biked down a long dirt path through farmers’ fields. I finished my day in a conservation area near Picton. My ride toward Kingston the next morning was along a quite stretch of the Waterfront Trail. I stayed in a motel in Kingston. The next day, I biked past the military base and took the ferry on the edge of Kingston. Gananoque and Brockville are beautiful towns. I camped at Crysler Park Marina. On my fifth day, I experienced the best of the Waterfront Trail. I rode through Upper Canada Village and crossed a series of islands connected by Long Sault Highway. I ate lunch by the marina in Cornwall then pedalled on to Glengarry Campground near Lancaster where I, again, camped on the river. On my sixth and final day, I rode into Montreal. I arrived at an AirBnB in the city and went to three restaurants. I took the train back to Toronto the next day.
